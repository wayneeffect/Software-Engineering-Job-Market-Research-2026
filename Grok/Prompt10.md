**The global software market** (the economic ecosystem that software engineering powers) is currently valued in the **$700–950 billion range** for 2026, with broader IT software spending (including infrastructure and applications) exceeding **$1.4 trillion**.

### Current Market Size (2026 Estimates)
Different research firms define the “software market” slightly differently, leading to some variation:

- **Broad global software market**: Approximately **$700–920 billion** in 2026.
  - Grand View Research and others had earlier estimates around $730B for 2024, growing rapidly.
  - Precedence Research projects ~$921 billion for 2026.
- **Gartner IT Software Spending** (most authoritative for enterprise context): **$1.4+ trillion in 2026**, growing 14.7–15.1% year-over-year. This covers application + infrastructure software and is a key indicator for the industry software engineering serves.
- **Software development-specific segments**: Smaller subsets like custom software development are in the $50–60B+ range, while engineering software (CAD/PLM etc.) is around $58B.

### Projections and Growth
The market is expected to grow strongly, driven by cloud adoption, AI integration, digital transformation, and enterprise software demand.

- **By 2030–2033**: Many forecasts point to **$1–1.5 trillion+** for the core software market, with some broader estimates reaching higher.
- **By 2035**: Projections range from **$2.2–2.5 trillion** in optimistic scenarios (CAGR ~11–12%).
- **CAGR**: Typically **8–12%** for the overall software market through the early 2030s, with higher rates (15–25%+) in AI, cloud, and custom development segments.

**Key growth drivers in 2026 and beyond**:
- AI and generative AI tools (very high growth).
- Cloud-native and enterprise software (ERP, CRM, cybersecurity).
- Digital transformation across industries.
- Increased demand for scalable, secure, and observable systems — all areas where software engineers deliver value.

**Note**: These figures represent **market revenue/spending**, not the job market itself. The software engineering *profession* benefits from this expansion, with strong long-term demand for skilled engineers despite short-term fluctuations in hiring.

In summary, the software market is already a massive, multi-trillion-dollar (when including related IT spending) industry and is projected to roughly double or more by the mid-2030s, fueled heavily by AI and cloud technologies. This underscores excellent long-term opportunities in software engineering. Figures can vary by source and exact scope, but the upward trajectory is consistent across major analysts like Gartner, Statista, and Precedence Research.
